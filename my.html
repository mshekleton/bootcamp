What is NFC and how does it work

Near-Field Communication (NFC) is a method for communicating over a short distance, typically less than an inch, between two objects. One of the most familiar examples of this is touchless payment like Android or Apple Pay. In this article, we will be discussing communication between a smartphone or a programmer and a an NFC "tag". An NFC tag is a passive RF circuit that is powered by a phone's radio emissions. This means that passive tags do not contain an batteries, which means their lifespan is essentially indefinite and they take of very little space. Most modern smartphones are NFC enabled, and don't require any third party applications needed to read the tag. Tags can hold records that prompt the phone to take actions like adding a contact, connecting to a WiFi network, or launching a website in the browser.
